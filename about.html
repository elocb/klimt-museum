About This Project

This digital museum was created as part of a Digital Humanities course project. The goal was to explore how digital tools can be used to organize and present cultural artifacts in engaging and accessible ways.

The focus of this virtual exhibition is the work of Gustav Klimt, one of the most iconic artists of the early 20th century. The collection brings together ten of his most well-known paintings, offering brief descriptions, historical context, and visual details.

The data for this site is hosted in a collaborative Google Sheet and fetched using a live JSON API. The design emphasizes clarity and simplicity, while small visual enhancements such as animations and zoom effects aim to make the user experience more engaging.

This project reflects the intersection of art, history, and digital design — inviting users to explore the legacy of Klimt in a modern format.
