About Gustav Klimt

Gustav Klimt (1862–1918) was an Austrian symbolist painter, known for his bold, decorative style and fascination with the human form. He was a central figure in the Vienna Secession movement and one of the most prominent artists of the Art Nouveau era.

His most famous works combine gold leaf, eroticism, and mythological or allegorical themes, as seen in masterpieces like The Kiss and Judith I. Klimt’s work continues to influence contemporary art and design through its sensuality and visual richness.
